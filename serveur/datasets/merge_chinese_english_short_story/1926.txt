Why is the horse's face long and the cat's face round? The horse is a herbivore. To facilitate the grinding of the grass, a row of molars has been grown, so that the face becomes longer. Cats are carnivores. They do not grind molars for food, so their jaws must be strong so they can withstand chewing over and over again. In order to support the sturdy chin, the cat's cheekbones are wide, strong, and muscular, with stretches on both sides. So the cat 's face is round. Cats are carnivores.
